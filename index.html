Top 3 Best Carinderia in Pasay

Food & Travel · BSTM 603

Have you ever been in Pasay? Behind the luxury hotels and big malls, the true heart of the city beats in its small local eateries — where the best Filipino food is also the most affordable.

1
Kambal Eatery
Cabrera St. Corner Vergel, Pasay

Known for Kapampangan specialties with fast turnover — often selling out by afternoon. Features Sisig, Menudo, Betute Tugak (stuffed frogs), and Adobong Kamaru (mole crickets in vinegar and garlic).

2
Libertad Carinderia (Cartimar)
Libertad Market, Pasay

Unnamed stalls offering authentic daily-cooked Filipino meals. No fixed menu — also serves international dishes like sushi and dumplings. Most popular among travelers and local residents.

3
MJ Carinderia
F.B. Harrison Street, Pasay City

Daily home-made dishes and meryenda specialties including Puto, Maja, and Bilo-Bilo (glutinous rice balls). Located near Star City, approximately 15–20 minutes from Roxas Boulevard.

Food Culture · Essay · BSTM 603

"Chimken Joy"

The sight of a gold-and-red logo paired with the aroma of fried chicken is a familiar experience that resonates deeply within Filipino households. Jollibee has transcended its status as a mere fast-food chain to become a cultural landmark, representing comfort and celebration for millions. For many, a meal consisting of Chimkenjoy, spaghetti, fries, and a cold drink is not just food — it is a symbol of achievement and familial bonding.

At the heart of this experience is the "Chickenjoy," often affectionately referred to by children as "chimken." This specific combination of crispy fried chicken, sweet-style spaghetti, and fries has become the standard traditional reward for Filipino children. Whether it is celebrating a high grade on a report card, a birthday, or simply a successful week at school, a trip to Jollibee is the primary incentive used by parents to motivate and reward their children.

The appeal lies in the unique flavor profile — the savory, juice-filled chicken paired with a distinctively sweet spaghetti sauce, specifically tailored to the Filipino palate. This meal, accompanied by a refreshing glass of Coke, creates a sensory experience that children associate with happiness and positive reinforcement.

In conclusion, Jollibee serves as more than just a quick-service restaurant — it is a staple of Filipino childhood and family traditions. By functioning as a universal reward for small and large milestones, the "Chickenjoy" meal has cemented itself in the local culture.

Photography · Essay · NAIA Terminal

Hello, Goodbye

Kervin Kyle Lubayan  ·  BSTM 603  ·  2024

Photo 1 — Hello, Goodbye (January 28, 2024)

Photo taken by Kervin Kyle Lubayan at the NAIA Terminal runway on January 28, 2024

On January 28, 2024, I stood on the NAIA Terminal runway and watched two planes cross paths. This moment made me realize that life is often a mix of endings and beginnings. To me, a departing plane represents a difficult choice — the "quiet bravery" of people leaving their families and personal happiness behind to work for a better future.

On the other hand, an arriving plane is full of joy. It represents the end of a long, lonely distance and the excitement of finally coming home to loved ones. Standing there as a ground crew member, I saw that every goodbye is just the start of a new story.

"I stood there between the two, watching the sky trade one story for another."

Photo 2 — Night Boarding Aircraft (July 28, 2024)

Why do we find ourselves mesmerized by the sight of a steel giant breathing under the glow of floodlights? For a third-year college student, this scene is more than a logistical transition — it is a visual metaphor for the threshold between the known and the unknown.

"Night Boarding Aircraft" is a symbol of transition that mirrors my own academic journey. Progress is rarely made in the bright light of day alone — it happens in the late hours, in the shadows and the grit of the "night shift" of our lives.

Standing within the expansive bay of NAIA, I was struck by the stillness of the asphalt stretching toward the fading light. Even as the natural light wanes, the presence of the workers who remain disciplined long after the sun dips below the horizon highlights profound persistence.

Lubayan, Kervin Kyle A.  ·  May 25, 2026

The global travel landscape has undergone profound transformation over the decade, transitioning from a period of high-speed mass tourism to more intentional and technology-driven experiences.

Top 3 Best Carinderia in Pasay

Lubayan, Kervin Kyle  ·  Food Lover's Guide

Have you ever been in Pasay? Pasay City, known as the Philippines' "Travel City," serves as the gateway due to the Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA). Behind the luxurious places and high-end restaurants, some people look for affordable but best home-made dishes.

1 — Kambal Eatery

Cabrera St. Corner Vergel, Pasay City

Known for their Kapampangan dishes and fast turnover, often selling out by afternoon. Popular menu items include Sisig, Menudo, Betute Tugak (stuffed frogs), and Adobong Kamaru (mole crickets sautéed in vinegar and garlic).

2 — Local Carinderia in Libertad (Cartimar)

Libertad Market Area, Pasay City

Unnamed stalls offering authentic, daily cooked Filipino meals — most popular among travelers and local residents. No fixed menus, and they also offer international dishes such as sushi and dumplings.

3 — MJ Carinderia

F.B. Harrison Street, Pasay City, Metro Manila

MJ Carinderia offers daily home-made dishes and meryenda specialties such as Puto, Maja, and Bilo-Bilo (glutinous rice balls). Located near Star City along Roxas Boulevard.

"In a city known for travel, sometimes the best journey is simply finding a great meal at a local stall."
